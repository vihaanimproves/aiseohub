GEO Basics

GEO is the discipline of optimising your business so that AI tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity cite and recommend you when users ask relevant questions. Unlike traditional SEO — which helps your pages rank in a list of results — GEO makes your business the answer that AI gives directly, by name.

No — and this is worth being precise about. Traditional SEO optimises for crawlers that index and rank web pages based on relevance signals. GEO optimises for large language models that synthesise information and recommend businesses based on trust, authority depth, and citation consistency across sources.

There's overlap — strong SEO foundation helps GEO — but the specific signals, content formats, and platforms that matter are different. A business could rank #1 on Google and still be completely invisible in AI answers. We've seen it repeatedly.

Our primary focus is ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Perplexity — the three tools with the highest local search query volume. We also monitor Microsoft Copilot as it expands.

The good news: the underlying signals that make you visible in one AI tool tend to carry across all of them. Building genuine category authority is not platform-specific — it compounds across the whole AI search ecosystem.

AI language models are trained on vast datasets and then updated with web data retrieval. When recommending a business, they weight several factors: how often a business is mentioned across trusted sources, the depth and consistency of information available about them, third-party endorsements and citations from credible platforms, and whether that information is structured in a way the model can parse and verify.

Businesses that appear frequently, consistently, and authoritatively across the sources AI models trust get recommended. Businesses without that depth — regardless of how good they actually are — simply don't appear.

They're complementary. GEO doesn't replace SEO — and we'd never recommend abandoning a working SEO strategy. We work alongside your existing team or agency without disrupting what they're doing.

In fact, many of the content infrastructure improvements we make for GEO purposes tend to strengthen traditional SEO performance as a side effect. It's genuinely additive in most cases.

Results & Timelines

Typically 60–90 days for measurable movement in AI mention frequency — meaning your brand starts appearing in AI responses for queries it didn't appear in before. Full category authority, where you're consistently recommended across a broad set of relevant queries, usually takes around 6 months.

We'll set specific baseline metrics at the start of the engagement so you always have a clear before/after picture. We don't deal in vague "brand awareness" outcomes.

We use structured query testing — a repeatable monthly framework where we ask ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity hundreds of queries relevant to your category and location, then log every response. We track: how often your brand appears, the specific context it appears in (recommended, mentioned, cited), which queries you're winning vs. competitors, and movement over time.

You receive a plain-English monthly report. No jargon, no spreadsheets you can't interpret. Just clear movement data and what we're doing about it.

We don't guarantee specific mention counts — no credible agency should, because AI model behaviour has variables outside anyone's direct control. What we do commit to is measurable, documented movement against your baseline within 90 days, for businesses in categories where GEO is applicable. If we don't believe we can move the needle for your specific situation, we'll tell you before you sign anything.

Concretely: a local legal practice going from appearing in 2 of 20 test queries to 14 of 20 over 90 days. A home services business getting calls from customers who say "ChatGPT recommended you."
