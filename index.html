What's changing

Search behaviour is shifting faster
than most businesses realise.

For two decades, "getting found online" meant ranking on Google. That model is still relevant — but it's no longer complete. A parallel discovery channel has emerged, and it operates on entirely different logic.

When someone opens ChatGPT or Gemini and types:

"Best accountant in Mumbai"
"Trusted physiotherapist in Bangalore"
"Recommended contractor near me"

They get a direct answer — not a list of links. A shortlist of specific businesses, recommended by name. No ads. No sponsored placements. The criteria for appearing are different from Google's ranking signals, and most businesses have never thought about them.

51%

The 2025 Imperva Bad Bot Report found that more than half of all internet traffic is now automated — bots and AI crawlers indexing the web for generative engines. The web is being read by machines at a scale that has no historical precedent. The businesses those machines find and trust will be the ones that get recommended.

🔄

A new discovery layer sits above conventional search

AI tools don't rank pages — they synthesise answers and cite sources. The businesses that appear are the ones that have built authority in the sources AI models trust. It's a different game from SEO, running in parallel right now.

⏳

Early positions in AI search are forming now

AI models develop citation tendencies based on authority signals they encounter early. Businesses building that presence now will have a position that becomes harder to contest as the channel matures.

🔦

Most businesses have no idea where they stand

There's no dashboard, no alert for AI visibility. Right now a competitor may be recommended every time someone asks an AI tool a question that's directly relevant to your business. Most owners have never checked.

Generative Engine Optimization

GEO is the discipline of building
visibility in AI-driven search.

Just as SEO emerged to address Google's dominance, GEO is emerging to address the rise of AI as a discovery and recommendation layer. The two disciplines overlap — but they aren't the same thing, and conflating them is how businesses end up invisible in AI answers despite strong Google rankings.

01

Understanding where you currently stand

Before anything else, it's worth knowing how your business actually appears when AI tools field relevant queries in your market. What are potential customers asking? Who's being named in your place? Most businesses haven't checked.

Focus: Honest baseline, not assumptions.
02

Building the signals AI models rely on

AI models cite businesses with consistent, credible, verifiable information across the sources they trust — industry publications, Q&A platforms, structured data. These signals are distinct from what drives Google rankings.

Focus: Authority depth, not keyword density.
03

Tracking visibility over time

AI search has no rankings page. Visibility is measured by running structured query sets monthly and logging how often a business is cited, in what context, relative to local competitors. Methodical, not magic.

Focus: Measurable movement, not vague awareness.
