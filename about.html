Generative Engine Optimization

What GEO actually is — and how it differs from SEO.

GEO is not SEO with a new name. It's a separate discipline — one that addresses how large language models discover, evaluate, and recommend businesses. Understanding the difference is the first step to doing anything about it.

Traditional SEO
  • Optimises for search engine crawlers
  • Goal: rank a page higher in results
  • Measured by position, clicks, CTR
  • User still has to choose your link
  • Affected by algorithm updates
  • Highly competitive on popular keywords
vs
Generative Engine Optimization
  • Optimises for large language model trust signals
  • Goal: become the cited, recommended source
  • Measured by AI mention frequency and context
  • AI recommends your business directly by name
  • Affected by data freshness and authority depth
  • Early movers have a meaningful compounding advantage

"Google ranks pages. AI recommends businesses. The criteria for appearing are entirely different."

How I Work

What the audit actually involves.

This is the process I follow for every business in the early access program. It's methodical, not automated, and it produces a real picture — not a dashboard full of numbers that don't mean anything.

Step 01

Query Testing

I run structured queries across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity for your specific category and location. The goal is a clear picture of what's being returned, who appears, and where your business currently stands relative to the competition.

Output: A documented snapshot of your current AI search presence
Step 02

Pattern Analysis

Based on the audit, I map what correlates with visibility in your space — which content types, platforms, and signals the AI tools appear to rely on most when generating answers for businesses like yours.

Output: A plain-English breakdown of what's working and what's absent
Step 03

Content and Citation Work

Building the signals that AI models rely on — structured content, citations on relevant platforms, and consistent information across the sources these tools index. This is the actual work, not just another report.

Output: Real work done, not just recommendations
Step 04

Monthly Tracking

I re-run the same query set each month and document what changed. You see exactly what moved, what didn't, and what the next step is. No vague progress updates — just the raw results of the same tests, repeated consistently.

Output: Monthly visibility report, plain English, no jargon
